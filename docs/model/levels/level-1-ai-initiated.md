# Level 1 — AI Initiated  

> **License:** CC BY-SA 4.0  
> https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/

## Tool Adoption Without Structural Change

This is basically the “we gave everyone copilots” phase.

Developers start using AI for autocomplete, quick snippets, explaining weird code, and maybe generating tests when they’re tired. It’s useful — nobody wants to go back to pure manual typing — but the overall way software gets built doesn’t really change.

Humans still decide what to build, how to design it, how to split the work, and whether the result is acceptable. AI just helps along the edges.

If you looked only at release cadence or planning meetings, you might not even notice AI was introduced.

---

## Core Characteristics

- Typing is faster, but decision-making is not  
- Benefits depend a lot on who bothers to use the tools  
- Planning, reviews, approvals — same as before  
- Adoption varies wildly across teams  
- AI output treated as “helpful suggestions,” not something you trust blindly  

---

## Organizational Signals

What you typically see at this stage:

- Individuals experimenting on their own  
- Different teams picking different tools (sometimes secretly 😅)  
- Security/legal teams scrambling to catch up  
- Leadership sensing opportunity but not having a plan yet  

It can feel exciting and chaotic at the same time.

---

## Primary Bottleneck

**Human attention**

AI reduces the grunt work of typing, but someone still has to think through the problem, make trade-offs, and verify everything. That’s where most of the time goes anyway.

---

## Common Failure Mode

### Copilot Theater

Lots of visible activity, very little measurable impact.

Typical symptoms:

- Endless conversations about AI adoption  
- Impressive one-off productivity stories  
- Delivery timelines mostly unchanged  
- Leaders are assuming “we’re transforming” because usage metrics look high  

Basically… looks fast, moves slow.

---

## Transition Requirements to Level 2

To move past this phase, organizations usually need to get intentional:

- Standardize on a small set of tools instead of a free-for-all  
- Teach people how to use AI for larger tasks, not just snippets  
- Put basic guardrails in place for data/security  
- Realize the payoff comes from changing workflows, not just installing tools  

Otherwise you just stay in the “helpful autocomplete” zone forever.
