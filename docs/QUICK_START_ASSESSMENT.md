# Quick Start Assessment Guide

Use this guide to perform a rapid maturity assessment of your organization in 30–60 minutes.

This is a lightweight entry point into the full OAITMM framework.

---

## Step 1 — Identify Target Scope

Choose the system or organizational boundary to assess:

- Entire engineering organization
- Business unit or product line
- Platform team
- AI initiatives portfolio
- Specific system or domain

Avoid mixing unrelated domains in one assessment.

---

## Step 2 — Determine Observed Operating Level

Using the level descriptions:

👉 docs/model/levels/

Select the level that best describes how work is actually performed today.

Focus on:

- How tasks are executed
- Role of AI and automation
- Human involvement in coding and review
- Nature of artifacts (code vs specs vs outcomes)

Record the provisional level (1–5).

---

## Step 3 — Score Capability Pillars

Evaluate each pillar using the scoring guidance:

👉 docs/model/assessment/pillars/

Score each dimension from 0 to 4 based on evidence.

Use organizational reality, not aspirational plans.

Calculate:

- Pillar scores
- Overall capability profile
- Strengths and weaknesses

---

## Step 4 — Evaluate Stage Gates

Assess readiness constraints:

👉 docs/model/assessment/gates/

Determine whether the organization satisfies:

- Safety requirements
- Evaluation adequacy
- Governance controls
- Recoverability
- Domain suitability

Gates may cap effective maturity regardless of capability.

---

## Step 5 — Determine Effective Maturity

Combine:

- Observed operating level
- Capability pillar scores
- Stage gate outcomes

Use the unified scoring model:

👉 docs/model/assessment/scoring-model.md

---

## Step 6 — Interpret Results

Identify:

- True maturity level
- Bottlenecks preventing advancement
- Key risks
- High-leverage improvement areas
- Recommended next moves

Examples:

👉 docs/model/assessment/simulated-examples.md

---

## Recommended Assessment Team

Include cross-functional perspectives:

- Engineering leadership
- Platform/DevEx representatives
- Product or architecture stakeholders
- Security or governance participants
- AI initiative owners

Avoid single-person assessments.

---

## When To Use Full Framework

Use the detailed assessment when:

- Planning transformation programs
- Making major investments
- Benchmarking organizations
- Conducting external reviews
- Producing formal reports

---

## Output Format

A useful assessment report typically includes:

- Scope definition
- Effective maturity level
- Pillar scorecard
- Gate findings
- Key risks
- Recommended actions
- Follow-up timeline

---

## Reassessment Cadence

Suggested frequency:

- Rapidly evolving organizations: quarterly
- Stable environments: biannually
- Major transformation programs: milestone-based
